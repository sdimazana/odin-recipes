MILK PUDDING

INGREDIENTS
  • 140 grams granulated sugar
  • 400 mL whole milk
  • 100 mL heavy whipping cream
  • 1/2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • Water
  • Custard molds (five 220-mL molds, six 150-mL molds, or seven 120-mL molds)
STEPS
  1. Gather all the ingredients. You will have to be prepared to complete each step quickly once you begin cooking.
  2. Preheat the oven to 300ºF. Boil 3 to 4 cups of water and keep it hot. This water will be used for the caramel sauce and bain-marie.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed small saucepan, combine 70 g of sugar and 1 tbsp of room temperature water. Turn the heat to medium low and let the sugar dissolve without stirring. Tilt the pot to help dissolve the sugar.
  4. Once the sugar has dissolved, increase the heat to medium. Swirl the saucepan occasionally but do not mix it with a utensil.
  5. When the sugar starts to caramelize, you will see lots of small bubbles. It will slowly turn brown on the edges of the pan. Swirl the pot to incorporate the dark-colored caramelized sugar into the light-colored sugar. Soon, the bubbles will become larger as the sugar mixture caramelizes more and turns an amber color.
  6. The caramelized sugar will continue to turn darker and darker. Keep swirling the pot and watch it closely. After the caramel sauce starts smoking, wait a tiny bit longer until it reaches an optimal dark caramel color. Then turn off the heat.
  7. Immediately pour a portion of the 1 tbsp hot water onto a spatula into the caramel pot (so your hand is not directly above the pot). The mixture will boil vigorously and sputter, so be careful not to let it splatter on you. Once this has subsided, slowly add the rest of the hot water. Swirl the saucepan to combine.
  8. Immediately pour equal portions of the caramel sauce into the molds. Do not worry if the caramel hardens before you can coat the entire mold. When you add the hot milk later, the caramel will melt and coat the whole mold.
  1. Beat three large eggs in a separate bowl until smooth. Add 70 grams of sugar and whisk till combined.
  2. In a small saucepan, whisk together 400 mL whole milk, 100 mL heavy whipping cream, and 1/2 tsp pure vanilla extract.
  3. Bring the milk mixture to a simmer on medium low heat.
  4. Once small bubbles appear on the edges of the saucepan, remove the pan from the heat.
  5. While whisking continuously, add 1/3 of the hot milk mixture to the bowl with the egg mixture. Continue to slowly add the rest of the milk mixture while whisking continuously.
  6. Strain the mixture using a fine mesh sieve.
  7. Evenly divide the mixture among the molds.
  8. Pop the bubbles on the surface of the custard by quickly touching them with a flame from a lighter or a toothpick.
  9. Place the molds in a baking pan that is at least 2 inches deep. Add enough hot water to the pan so that the water comes up halfway to the molds.
  10. Bake the pudding at 300ºF for 35-45 minutes or until the custard is set.
  11. You can check that the pudding is done by jiggling it. If it sloshes like liquid, it is not done. Once they are done, let them cool completely on a wire rack.
  12. Refrigerate for 2-3 hours and enjoy.
