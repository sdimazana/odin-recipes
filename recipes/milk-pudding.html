Milk Pudding

Description

Milk pudding is a popular Japanese dessert that is deliciously smooth, creamy, and sweet.

Ingredients

Caramel Sauce

  • 70 grams of granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp room temperature water
  • 1 tbsp hot water
  • Custard molds. Any of the below options work:
    • Five 220-ml molds
    • Six 150-ml molds
    • Seven 120-ml molds

Custard

  • 400 mL of whole milk
  • 100 mL of heavy whipping cream
  • 1/2 tsp of pure vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs (50 grams each without shell)
  • 70 grams of granulated sugar

Bain-Marie

  • 3-4 cups of hot water

Steps

Making the caramel sauce

  1. Preheat the oven to 300째F (150째C). For a convection oven, reduce the cooking temperature by 25째F (15째C).
  2. In a heavy-bottomed small saucepan, combine 70 grams of sugar and 1 tbsp of room temperature water. Avoid using nonstick saucepans. They tend to have thin bottoms and their inner pot color makes it hard to see the caramel color.
  3. On medium low heat, let the sugar dissolve completely. Do not touch or stir the mixture. Let the sugar turn to liquid. If necessary, tilt the pot occasionally to help dissolve the sugar.
  4. Start boiling 3-4 cups of hot water in a kettle and keep it hot.
  5. Once the sugar has fully dissolved, increase the heat to medium or medium high. Swirl the saucepan once in a while to stir the mixture, but do NOT use a utensil or the sugar will crystalize. This should take about 5 minutes.
  6. When the sugar starts to caramelize, you will see lots of small bubbles. It will slowly turn brown on the edges of the pan. Swirl the pot to incorporate the dark-colored caramelized sugar into the light-colored sugar. Soon the bubbles will become larger as the sugar mixture caramelizes more and turns an amber color.
  7. The caramelized sugar will continue to turn darker and darker. Keep swirling the pot and watch it closely. Once the caramel sauce starts smoking, wait a tiny bit longer until it reaches an optimal dark caramel color. Then, turn off the heat.
  8. Hold a spatula over the pot. Pour a portion of the 1 tbsp of hot water on the spatula and into the caramel pot. The mixture will boil vigorously and sputter, so take care not to let it splatter on you. Once this has subsided, slowly add the rest of the hot water. Swirl the saucepan to combine.
  9. As soon as the hot caramel sauce is done, pour it directly into the molds. It will cool and harden quickly. Aim for a thin layer of caramel about 3 mm thick.

Making the custard

  1. In a small saucepan, combine 400 mL of whole milk, 100 mL of heavy whipping cream, and 1/2 tsp of pure vanilla extract.
  2. Whisk it all together. Turn the heat on to medium low and bring the milk mixture to a simmer.
  3. Crack 3 eggs into a large bowl and beat with a whisk until smooth.
  4. Add 70 grams of sugar to the eggs and mix until everything is combined.
  5. When small bubbles start to appear around the edges of the saucepan, turn off the heat.
  6. While whisking continulously, slowly add 1/3 of the hot milk mixture into the egg mixture. Then, continue to add the rest of the milk mixture slowly, again whisking continulously. Mix it all together. Add the hot milk mixture slowly to keep the eggs from curdling.
  7. Set a fine-mesh sieve over another bowl. Strain the custard mixture through the sieve to remove any lumps.
  8. Evenly divide the custard mixture among the molds. It's important that all of your molds have an equal volume of custard so the cooking time will be the same.
  9. Using a lighter, burn off the bubbles on the surface of the custard by quickly touching them with the flame. You can also pop the bubbles with a toothpick or scoop them off with a spoon, but this is time- consuming.

Baking the custard

  1. Place the molds in a baking dish that is at least 2.5 cm deep so we can add hot water to it. Then put the dish on a baking sheet. The baking sheet makes it easier to transfer the baking dish into the oven after we pour hot water into it. Pour enough hot water so that the molds are half submerged.
  2. Place the baking dish into the preheated oven and bake for 35-45 minutes or until the custard is set. If you are using larger or thicker molds, you may need to cook it for 45-60 minutes.
  3. Check for doneness by tilting the molds. If it is done cooking, the surface of the pudding will be slightly wobbly but no longer liquid.
  4. Take out the molds from the water bath. Let cool completely on a wire rack.
  5. Once cooled, cover the molds with plastic or aluminum foil and refridgerate for 2-3 hours.

Serving the pudding

  1. Run a butter knife or offset spatula along the edges of the mold to separate the pudding from the mold.
  2. Place a serving plate face down on top of the mold.
  3. Flip to release the pudding and caramel sauce from its mold and onto the plate. If it does not fall from the mold, try gently shaking the mold on the plate.
