STRAWBERRY CUPCAKES

INGREDIENTS
  • 10 oz cake flour
  • 9 oz granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 large eggs at room temperature
  • 4 oz vegetable oil
  • 5 oz buttermilk at room temperature
  • 4 oz softened unsalted butter
  • 3 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 pint heavy cream
  • 1 cup sifted powdered sugar
  • 1 quart fresh strawberries
STEPS
  1. Preheat the oven to 350ºF and line two cupcake pans with paper liners.
  2. Combine 2 tsp of vanilla and the 5 oz of buttermilk in a bowl and set aside.
  3. Whisk the 2 eggs and 4 oz of vegetable oil in a separate bowl and set aside.
  4. Add the cake flour, granulated sugar, salt, baking powder, and baking soda to the bowl of a stand mixer. Mix using the paddle attachment at a medium-low speed.
  5. Add the softened butter into the bowl with the dry ingredients and mix on low until the mixture resembles wet sand.
  6. Pour in the mixture of vanilla and buttermilk from step 2 into the bowl of the stand mixer. Mix for 1 and a half minutes at medium speed (speed 4 on Kitchen Aid, speed 2 on Bosch). The batter will become fluffy and pale.
  7. Add the egg mixture in 3 parts and mix on low until fully combined.
  8. Fill the liners 2/3 of the way full with cupcake batter. This should be about 1.5 oz of batter per cupcake.
  9. Bake the cupcakes for 15-20 minutes or until they are just starting to turn golden brown. The center of the cupcake should bring back lightly when pressed.
  10. Let the cupcakes cool for 5 minutes in their pan before transferring to a wire rack.
  11. Add the pint of heavy cream to the bowl of a stand mixer and beat on high with the whisk attachment until the cream is frothy.
  12. Reduce the speed to medium and slowly add the cup of sifted powered sugar and 1 tsp vanilla extract into the cream.
  13. Whip until the cream has stiff peaks.
  14. Place the whipped cream in a piping bag and frost the cooled vanilla cupcakes.
  15. Place strawberries on top of the cupcake as desired.
NOTES
  • The closer your butter is to room temperature, the sooner the batter will take on the “wet sand” texture in step 5.
  • The author of this recipe, Liz Marek, recommends rotating the cupcakes halfway through their baking time in step 9 to get an even browning on the top of the cupcakes.
  • Brushing the fresh strawberries with a light layer of jam gives the fruit a shiny appearance and improves their sweetness! I recommend apricot marmalade or peach jam - anything with a golden brown color. I think these jams and marmalades add shine to the strawberries without drastically altering their color.
